# Reference: B2B Strategy Frameworks
# 5 Cypress Automation — Knowledge Base

These frameworks should be drawn upon when building strategy briefs, roadmaps, and content for B2B tech and medical clients. Use them to structure recommendations and give clients a mental model for their marketing.

---

## 1. The B2B Buyer Journey (for Long Sales Cycles)

B2B buyers — especially in tech and medical — don't buy impulsively. Map every marketing activity to a stage:

| Stage | Buyer Mindset | Marketing Goal | Best Channels |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Awareness** | "I have a problem" | Get found. Build trust. | SEO, LinkedIn, referrals |
| **Consideration** | "What are my options?" | Demonstrate expertise | Email nurture, case studies, demos |
| **Decision** | "Who do I trust to solve this?" | Remove friction, reduce risk | Proposals, ROI calculators, testimonials |
| **Retention** | "Am I getting value?" | Deepen relationship | Check-ins, reports, upsell campaigns |

**Key insight for 5 Cypress clients:** Most small B2B companies only market at the Decision stage. The biggest opportunity is usually building Awareness and Consideration infrastructure.

## 2. Positioning: The 3-Circle Framework

Good positioning lives at the intersection of:
1. **What you're uniquely good at** (internal strength)
2. **What your customers actually need** (real market demand)
3. **What competitors don't do well** (competitive gap)

Use this when building the Positioning section of any strategy brief.

---

## 3. The Message House

Structure client messaging in layers:
- **Foundation:** Core value proposition (one sentence)
- **Pillars (3):** The 3 main reasons someone should choose this client
- **Proof Points:** Specific evidence for each pillar (stats, case studies, testimonials)
- **Tagline (optional):** Memorable short phrase that captures the foundation

---

## 4. The 90-Day Marketing Sprint Framework

Use for all roadmaps:
- **Month 1 — Foundation:** Fix positioning, set up tracking, clean up email list, audit channels
- **Month 2 — Activation:** Launch primary campaign, begin content publishing, start outreach
- **Month 3 — Optimization:** Double down on what worked, cut what didn't, set next sprint goals

---

## 5. The Email Nurture Ladder

For B2B email sequences, escalate the ask gradually:

| Email | Ask Level | Example CTA |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 1-2 | No ask | Just read this / learn something |
| 3-4 | Micro ask | Reply with a thought / download resource |
| 5-6 | Soft ask | Book a 15-min call / request a demo |
| 7+ | Direct ask | Are you ready to move forward? |

---

## 6. The Content Pillars Model

For clients who need a sustainable content strategy, build 3-4 content pillars:
- Each pillar = one major theme they can speak to with authority
- Every piece of content maps to a pillar
- Pillar selection should be based on: audience pain points + client expertise + competitive whitespace

**For B2B Tech clients:** Common pillars — Industry trends, Product education, ROI/business case, Implementation & how-to

**For Medical clients:** Common pillars — Clinical outcomes, Compliance/regulatory, Workflow efficiency, Patient impact

---

## 7. The ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Framework

Define the ideal customer by:
- **Firmographic:** Industry, company size, geography, revenue
- **Technographic:** What tools/systems they already use
- **Psychographic:** Values, priorities, how they make decisions
- **Pain profile:** Specific problems they need solved
- **Trigger events:** What makes them start looking for a solution now?

Always define ICP before launching any campaign.
