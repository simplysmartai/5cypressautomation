## Executive Summary

MedSync Solutions is a patient intake SaaS built for the one segment of the market that the biggest platforms consistently underserve: the independent medical practice. The product has strong fundamentals — a 28% trial-to-paid conversion rate, EHR integrations, and word-of-mouth traction — but zero marketing infrastructure. No email nurture. No content. No SEO. No social presence.

The opportunity is clear: the digital patient intake market is growing at 13.7% CAGR toward $4.9B by 2031, and the dominant players (Phreesia, Clearwave) are actively alienating small practices with enterprise complexity, hardware requirements, and aggressive upsell behavior. MedSync's positioning — simple, fast, purpose-built for small practices — is a credible and defensible wedge.

The 90-day priority is threefold: (1) build the channels that generate consistent inbound leads, (2) capture the high-intent traffic that already exists but goes unmonetized, and (3) put in place the email infrastructure to convert the 640-person list into demo requests.

**Primary Goal:** 20+ qualified inbound demo requests/month by end of Month 3.

## Section 1 — Market Landscape

### 1a. Industry Overview

The digital patient intake software market was valued at **$1.76 billion in 2024** and is projected to reach **$4.92 billion by 2031** (CAGR: 13.7%). Growth is driven by:

- Post-COVID acceleration of contactless and patient self-service workflows  
- Increased regulatory pressure for HIPAA-compliant documentation  
- EHR digitization mandates increasing demand for bidirectional data sync  
- Mobile-first patient expectations: patients now expect to complete intake on their phone before arriving  
- AI and automation adoption: 85% of practice managers see AI as viable for administrative workflows in 2025  

**Key trend for MedSync:** Cloud-based, mobile-first tools dominate adoption in smaller practices because they eliminate infrastructure and IT dependencies. This is MedSync's native format — a structural advantage, not a feature parity issue.

**Regulatory context:** HIPAA compliance remains a baseline requirement. No major new regulations in 2026, but enforcement actions are rising — making "HIPAA-ready out of the box" an increasingly meaningful differentiator for practices that are nervous about compliance risk.

### 1b. Target Audience Research

**Who the buyer is:** Practice Manager or Office Administrator at an independent practice (1–10 physicians). Often the de facto IT decision-maker by default, not expertise. Approves or recommends software purchases under ~$1,000/month without physician sign-off.

**What they are actively experiencing in 2026:**
- CMS cut the physician payment conversion factor by 2.83% in 2025 — revenue pressure is acute
- Staffing shortages mean front-desk teams are asked to do more with less
- Administrative burden is the #1 reported driver of staff burnout in ambulatory care
- 70% of independent practices lack a comprehensive patient engagement strategy (AdvancedMD survey)
- Only 31% have a communication plan that covers the full patient journey

**How they talk about this problem:**
- "Our front desk is slammed" / "Check-in takes forever"
- "Patients show up without their paperwork" / "We're still on paper forms"
- "I can't afford a full IT setup" / "I need something that just works with [EHR]"
- "HIPAA compliance is a concern but I don't know where to start"
- They search for: *patient intake software*, *digital intake forms for medical practice*, *replace paper forms clinic*, *HIPAA intake forms*, *EHR check-in automation*

**Key insight:** These buyers are time-starved and skeptical. They've been burned by software that promised simplicity and delivered complexity. The biggest conversion barrier isn't price — it's trust and setup friction. MedSync's 14-day trial and "same-day setup" claim directly addresses this. All marketing must reinforce it.

### 1c. Competitive Analysis

| Competitor | Positioning | Key Weakness for Small Practices |
|------------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| **Phreesia** | Enterprise patient intake leader | Steep learning curve; upsells; poor small-practice customer service; optional ads in patient check-in; hardware dependencies |
| **Clearwave** | Self-service kiosk-based check-in | Requires physical kiosk hardware — wrong for small offices; specialty-focused |
| **Solutionreach** | Patient relationship + communication | Self-scheduling bugs; misleading contracts; upsell-heavy; more CRM than intake tool |
| **Klara** | Patient messaging + intake | Communication-focused, not workflow-focused; not purpose-built for intake efficiency |
| **Relatient** | Scheduling and engagement | Complex setup; part of larger platform; overkill for 1-5 physician practices |

**Gap analysis:** No category leader is explicitly positioning for the 1–10 physician independent practice with a "simple + fast + affordable" message. Phreesia dominates mindshare but actively alienates this segment. MedSync can own this position with consistent, targeted messaging.

**Review-based intel:** Users leave Phreesia and Solutionreach citing "too complex," "too expensive for our size," and "upsells constantly." These are warm prospects for MedSync's outreach.

### 1d. Channel Benchmarks (B2B Health Tech, SMB Buyer)

## Section 2 — Positioning & Messaging

### Positioning Statement

MedSync Solutions is the patient intake platform built specifically for independent medical practices — not scaled-down enterprise software, not overbuilt communication tools. For practice managers who need to modernize check-in without hiring IT or signing a complex contract, MedSync delivers a HIPAA-ready digital intake experience that installs in a day, syncs with their EHR from day one, and frees up their front desk immediately. Where Phreesia serves hospital systems, MedSync serves the practices everyone else overlooks.

### 3 Core Messages

**1. Built for practices your size — not hospital systems**  
Every feature is sized and priced for a 1–10 physician practice. No enterprise contracts. No kiosk hardware. No onboarding team required. Just the tool you need, working the way your practice actually works.

**2. Same-day setup. Patients onboarded before they walk in the door.**  
Digital intake, consent forms, and insurance verification — completed on the patient's phone before the appointment. Your front desk sees everything in real time. Setup takes one afternoon, not one quarter.

**3. HIPAA-ready from day one. No IT department needed.**  
Encrypted, compliant, and reviewed for HIPAA alignment — available without a security team, a dedicated IT vendor, or a compliance consultant. Check the box, protect your practice.

### Value Proposition (One Sentence)

> MedSync replaces paper intake chaos at independent medical practices with a mobile-first, HIPAA-ready system that sets up in a day — so your front desk can stop re-entering data and start focusing on patients.

### Messaging Hierarchy

```
Level 1 (Hook):    "Your front desk, upgraded."
Level 2 (Problem): "Independent practices are still running paper forms and re-entering data by hand."
Level 3 (Solution):"MedSync digitizes patient intake, consent, and insurance verification — before patients arrive."
Level 4 (Proof):   "Set up in one afternoon. Integrates with Epic, Athenahealth, DrChrono. 28% of practices that trial MedSync convert to paid."
Level 5 (CTA):     "Start your free 14-day trial →"
```

## Section 3 — 90-Day Marketing Roadmap

### Month 1 — Foundation (March 2026)
*Goal: Build the infrastructure. No campaigns run well without this.*

**Priority Actions:**

1. **Email list revival — segment and activate the 640-contact list**
   - Segment into: trial users who didn't convert, past demo requests, conference leads
   - Clean the list (remove bounces, flag inactives)
   - Set up Mailchimp or migrate to HubSpot email (already on free tier)
   - Deliverable: First email sent by end of Week 3 (see Month 2 nurture launch)

2. **Website conversion audit**
   - Does the homepage clearly state who this is for? Does the above-the-fold pass the 5-second test?
   - Is there a clear free trial CTA visible without scrolling on mobile?
   - Are there social proof elements (client logos, a single testimonial, trial conversion stat)?
   - Deliverable: Written audit + 5–7 specific copy/CTA recommendations

3. **Google Ads — baseline campaign setup**
   - Target 3 high-intent keyword clusters: *patient intake software*, *digital intake forms for clinics*, *HIPAA intake forms small practice*
   - Ad copy: Lead with free trial + same-day setup angle
   - Budget: $800–$1,000/month starting budget; optimize in Month 2
   - Deliverable: Campaign live by end of Month 1

4. **Case study capture — identify 2 willing clients**
   - Reach out to 3 best customer relationships for a quick 20-minute interview
   - Document: how they used to do intake, what changed, measurable outcome (time saved, no-shows reduced)
   - Format: 1-page PDF + condensed quote cards for web/email use
   - Deliverable: 1 published case study by end of Month 2

### Month 2 — Activation (April 2026)
*Goal: Launch nurture sequences and generate first inbound leads from owned channels.*

**Launch Email Nurture Sequence — Unconverted Trial Users**
- 5-email sequence targeting users who started a trial but didn't convert
- Angle: Remove objections, restate value, offer a 30-minute "setup call" instead of a hard sales call
- Expected outcome: 3–8% reactivation → 10–25 demo conversations from the existing list
- *Full campaign copy delivered separately as the second deliverable of this engagement*

**LinkedIn Activation — Low-Spend, High-Precision**
- Set up a posting cadence for Danielle's personal LinkedIn (2×/week)
- Content mix: one educational post (patient intake insight), one story/result post (practice impact)
- Goal: Build Danielle as a trusted voice for independent practice operations — not ads, just genuine presence
- Budget: $0 in Month 2; assess paid promotion in Month 3

**Google Ads Optimization**
- Review Month 1 performance: pause underperforming keywords, increase bids on converters
- Introduce 1 retargeting campaign for website visitors who didn't start a trial
- Expected: Cost per trial start under $120 by end of Month 2

**Community Infiltration**
- Identify 3 online communities where practice managers congregate (Facebook groups, MGMA forums, Kareo/Athenahealth user communities)
- Begin building a genuine presence (answer questions, don't pitch)
- Goal: 1–2 inbound warm leads from community engagement by Month 3

### Month 3 — Optimization & Scale (May 2026)
*Goal: Hit 20+ demo requests/month. Double down on what's working.*

**Email — Second Sequence: Cold/Warm Leads**
- 3-email sequence for conference leads and past demo requests (colder contacts)
- Angle: Lead with a useful insight, offer low-friction next step (not a demo — a checklist, a calculator, or a "how to evaluate intake software" guide)
- Introduce a lead magnet: *"5 Signs Your Practice Is Ready to Ditch Paper Intake"* — PDF guide, gated behind email capture on the website

**Scale Google Ads to $1,500/month**
- Introduce branded competitor campaign: "Phreesia alternative for small practices" / "Clearwave for small clinics"
- These capture buyers who are already researching and are actively dissatisfied

**Publish Second Case Study**
- Use to fuel email, LinkedIn, and homepage social proof sections
- Quote: specific stat (e.g., "We reduced check-in time from 18 minutes to 4 minutes.")

**SEO Groundwork (Long-Term)**
- Publish 2 foundational blog posts targeting high-intent keywords:
  - *"Best Patient Intake Software for Independent Medical Practices in 2026"*
  - *"How to Replace Paper Intake Forms at Your Clinic (Step-by-Step)"*
- These won't rank immediately but build domain authority for Q3–Q4 payoff

**Danielle Enablement — Sales + Marketing Alignment**
