Section 02

Market Landscape

Industry Overview

The digital patient intake software market was valued at $1.76 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach $4.92 billion by 2031 at a 13.7% CAGR. Growth is driven by post-COVID adoption of contactless workflows, rising HIPAA enforcement scrutiny, EHR integration mandates, and mobile-first patient expectations.

Cloud-based, mobile-first tools now dominate adoption among smaller practices — precisely because they eliminate hardware and IT dependencies. This is MedSync's native format, not a feature to add.

Key Trend

85% of practice managers see AI-driven workflow automation as viable in 2025. Practices that adopt digital intake now are building a foundation for further automation. MedSync positions clients ahead of this curve.

Target Buyer Profile

The primary decision-maker is the Practice Manager or Office Administrator at an independent practice (1–10 physicians). They approve software under ~$1,000/month without physician sign-off, are perpetually time-starved, and have been burned by software that promised simplicity and delivered complexity.

Section 03

Positioning & Messaging

Positioning Statement

MedSync Solutions is the patient intake platform built specifically for independent medical practices — not scaled-down enterprise software. For practice managers who need to modernize check-in without hiring IT, MedSync delivers HIPAA-ready digital intake that installs in a day, syncs with their EHR from day one, and frees up their front desk immediately. Where Phreesia serves hospital systems, MedSync serves the practices everyone else overlooks.

Value Proposition

"MedSync replaces paper intake chaos at independent medical practices with a mobile-first, HIPAA-ready system that sets up in a day — so your front desk can stop re-entering data and start focusing on patients."

Core Messages

1

Built for practices your size — not hospital systems

No enterprise contracts. No kiosk hardware. No onboarding team required. Every feature is sized and priced for a 1–10 physician practice.

2

Same-day setup. Patients onboarded before they walk in the door.

Digital intake, consent forms, and insurance verification — completed on the patient's phone before the appointment. Setup takes one afternoon, not one quarter.

3

HIPAA-ready from day one. No IT department needed.

Encrypted, compliant, reviewed for HIPAA alignment — without a security team, dedicated IT vendor, or compliance consultant.
