## What They Sell
- **Product/Service Name:** MedSync Intake — patient intake digitization platform
- **Description:** A SaaS platform that replaces paper intake forms and front-desk clipboard chaos at independent medical practices. Patients complete digital intake, consent forms, and insurance verification before their appointment — on their phone. The front desk sees a live dashboard. Integrates with major EHRs (Epic, Athenahealth, DrChrono).
- **Price Point / Deal Size:** $299–$699/month per practice location. Average contract ~$5,500/year.
- **Sales Cycle Length:** 3–8 weeks
- **How They Close:** Inbound demo request → 30-min product demo → free 14-day trial → close. Referrals from practice managers and office administrators are a strong secondary channel.

## Target Audience
- **Primary Buyer:** Practice Manager or Office Administrator at an independent medical practice
- **Secondary Buyer / Influencer:** The physician-owner or medical director who approves software spend
- **Company Profile of Ideal Customer:** Independent or small group medical practice (1–10 providers), primary care, specialty care (orthopedics, dermatology, OB-GYN), NOT large health systems or hospital networks
- **Key Pain Points Their Buyers Have:**
  - Front-desk staff overwhelmed by paper forms and data re-entry
  - Patient no-shows and late arrivals due to slow check-in
  - HIPAA-compliant digital forms are confusing/expensive to set up elsewhere
  - EHR integration is usually clunky or requires IT help they don't have
- **What Their Buyers Care About Most:** Time savings for front-desk staff, reduction in no-shows, ease of setup, HIPAA compliance, not needing IT

## Competitors
- **Main Competitors:**
  - Phreesia — large, enterprise-focused patient intake platform
  - Clearwave — mid-market, focused on automated patient registration
  - Relatient — patient engagement platform with intake features
  - Solutionreach — patient communication & scheduling for SMB practices
  - Klara — patient communication with some intake capability
- **How Client Differentiates:** MedSync is purpose-built for small independent practices (not hospitals), offers same-day setup, transparent pricing (competitors often require custom quotes), and a simpler UI than enterprise tools
- **Competitor Weaknesses to Exploit:** Phreesia/Clearwave are built for large groups; overkill and overpriced for small practices. Klara focuses on messaging, not intake workflow. MedSync wins on simplicity + EHR integration breadth for the SMB segment.
