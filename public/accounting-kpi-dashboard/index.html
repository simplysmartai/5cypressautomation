KPI Dashboards

What to track in your
accounting firm dashboard

Most accounting firms get their key metrics too late to act on them. Here is what to track, why it matters, and how a live dashboard changes the way partners make decisions.

The month-end information problem

Accounting firms are, ironically, often the last to have good financial visibility into their own practice. Revenue gets reviewed at month-end. Realization rate gets pulled when the billing run is closed. AR aging gets checked when cash flow starts to feel tight.

By the time that information surfaces, the decisions it should have informed were due two or three weeks ago. The partner who sees unbilled WIP at $180K on October 31 could have done something about it on October 14.

A practice KPI dashboard does not add new information — it surfaces what already exists in your systems (QuickBooks Online and your practice management platform) in real time, to every partner, without anyone having to run a report.

The four metrics that matter most

Realization rate

Billed fees ÷ potential fees at standard rates. The single most important measure of whether your firm is capturing the value it creates.

Why it matters live

Most firms can quote a rough number. Very few can tell you which engagements are dragging it down — until month-end, when it is too late to intervene.

Work in progress (WIP)

Time recorded but not yet billed. High WIP is money sitting on the shelf.

Why it matters live

Without a live view, WIP accumulates invisibly. By the time the billing run surfaces it, the client conversation about scope is harder to have.

AR aging

Outstanding receivables bucketed by age: 0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+.

Why it matters live

Aging AR is the single most predictable cash flow problem. Partners who see it weekly act on it. Partners who see it monthly are always surprised.

Billing trend

Month-over-month billed revenue versus the prior 6-month average.

Why it matters live

Revenue feels abstract until you can see the line move. A dashboard that shows the trend creates urgency around billing backlogs.

How a live accounting dashboard actually works

A well-built practice dashboard connects directly to QuickBooks Online via the QBO API and your practice management system — Karbon, TaxDome, Clio, or CCH. It runs on a sync schedule (every few hours, or on-demand) and serves data to a web-based panel accessible at a shared URL.

There is no spreadsheet involved. No one pulls a report. No one emails a CSV. The dashboard exists at a URL that partners can bookmark. It updates itself.

Optional features most firms add after the first month: alerting when WIP crosses a threshold, alerting when an AR bucket hits a dollar amount, and historical trend lines that show month-over-month movement without pulling old reports.

What firms do differently once they have live data

The behavioral change is more significant than most partners expect before they have it. Weekly partner meetings shift from "what are the numbers?" to "here is what the numbers say we should do." Billing backlogs get cleared faster because the WIP balance is visible every day, not once a month. AR follow-up happens sooner because the aging buckets are impossible to ignore.

The dashboard does not make decisions. It makes it harder to avoid them.

What we see in practice

Firms that implement live KPI dashboards consistently see a reduction in unbilled WIP within the first 60 days — not because anything changed operationally, but because visibility creates accountability. When partners can see the number every day, they prioritize billing differently.
