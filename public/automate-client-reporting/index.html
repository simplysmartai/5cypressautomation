Client Reporting

Stop rebuilding the
same report every month

Monthly client reports are accounting firms' most consistent time sink. Here is how automation eliminates the work without changing what clients receive.

The reporting treadmill

Ask a staff accountant at almost any small firm what they do between the 1st and the 5th of every month, and the answer is some version of the same thing: pull the QBO export, paste it into the branded template, adjust the formatting, generate the PDF, send the email. For each client. Every month.

This is not skilled work. It is repetition. And yet it consumes a meaningful portion of staff time every single month — time that cannot be billed, cannot be delegated to a junior without close review, and does not get easier with experience.

The information needed to produce these reports already exists in QuickBooks Online. The only reason a human is involved is that no one has connected the trigger (period close) to the output (branded report + delivery) without a person in the middle.

What automated client reporting looks like

1
QBO period closes

The system detects the accounting period close in QuickBooks Online.

2
Data pulled per client

Balance sheet, P&L, and any custom metrics are pulled via QBO API for each client automatically.

3
Report generated

A branded PDF and a live web dashboard are generated — no templates to fill in, no exports to format.

4
Optional: AI narrative

An AI-written plain-English summary of the period highlights is added to the report (optional, partner-reviewed).

5
Partner review (optional)

Before delivery, a one-click approval step lets the partner review and approve — or override — any report.

6
Delivered by email

The report is sent to the client by email. Delivery is logged with timestamp. No one builds anything manually.

What the partner keeps control of

Automated reporting does not mean unsupervised reporting. The system is built with a configurable review step: before any report goes out, the partner receives a notification and a one-click approval link. If everything looks right, approve and the email sends. If something needs adjustment, edit the draft before it delivers.

Most firms turn off the mandatory review step after the first quarter, once they have confidence in the output. Some keep it for high-value clients or any month where significant advisory context needs to be added.

What clients receive

From the client perspective, the experience is the same — or better. They receive a professionally formatted PDF and a link to a live web dashboard showing their financials updated through the prior period. The email arrives reliably before the 5th of the month instead of whenever someone got around to it.

The optional AI narrative summary is written in plain English — not accounting jargon — and highlights the three or four most meaningful numbers from the period. Clients who previously ignored reports start reading them.

Time math

A firm with 20 monthly reporting clients typically spends 8-15 hours per month on manual report production. At a fully-loaded staff cost of $35-50/hr, that is $280-$750 per month in labor for work that produces zero revenue. The automation pays for itself in the first quarter.
