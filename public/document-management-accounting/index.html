Document Intake

End tax season
document chaos

Clients uploading files to whoever responds first. Staff sorting and renaming all day. Documents in the wrong folder. There is a better way — and it takes less than three weeks to set up.

What document collection actually looks like at most small firms

Every January and February, the same thing happens at small accounting and tax firms across the country. Clients start sending documents. They send them to whoever they have a relationship with — a partner, a staff accountant, the front desk. They send them by email attachment. They send them in multiple emails, weeks apart, with no subject line organization.

On the firm side, someone is opening those emails, downloading the attachments, renaming them to some approximation of a file naming convention, and manually moving them into job folders inside Karbon, TaxDome, or SharePoint. When the folder structure is wrong, documents end up associated with the wrong client. When staff are overwhelmed, documents sit in inboxes waiting.

This is not a problem that gets better by asking clients to be more organized. It is a process problem, and it has a process solution.

How an AI-powered intake system works

An automated document intake system replaces the email-and-sort workflow with a structured upload portal. Clients receive a simple, branded link — no software to install, no account to create. They drag files in. Everything else is automatic.

Every file that enters the portal is scanned by OpenAI Vision, which reads the document and identifies what it is. The classification is specific enough to distinguish between a 1099-DIV and a 1099-INT, between a bank statement and a brokerage statement, between a W-2 from one employer and a W-2 from another. Once classified, the file is routed to the correct job folder in your practice management system — with proper naming conventions applied automatically.

The result is a clean incoming tray instead of a chaotic inbox. Staff see what came in, where it went, and what is still missing. No sorting. No renaming. No hunting through inboxes.

The missing document tracker

One of the most time-consuming parts of tax season is chasing clients for documents they have not yet submitted. Every year, staff spend hours looking across job folders trying to figure out who still needs to send what.

An automated intake system solves this by tracking submission status per client. The missing document tracker shows a simple list: which clients have submitted a complete package, which are partial, and what is still expected. This eliminates the manual status-check workflow entirely.

Tax season impact

Firms preparing 200+ returns consistently recover 15-25 hours of staff time during peak season after implementing automated document intake. The hours come from two places: eliminating manual sorting and renaming, and eliminating status checks on who has submitted what.
